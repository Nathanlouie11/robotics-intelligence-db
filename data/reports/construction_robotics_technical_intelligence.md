## 2. TECHNICAL HURDLES & LIMITATIONS

**1. Energy Density for Untethered Operation**
- **Challenge**: Battery capacity limiting continuous heavy-lift operations
- **Current State**: 4-6 hours operational time (20-30 kWh packs) for 50kg+ payload robots
- **Target State**: 10-12 hour shift duration (50+ kWh) with <30 minute charging
- **Who's Working On It**: Boston Dynamics (Spot), Built Robotics, ETH Zurich ( ANYmal research)
- **Estimated Timeline**: 2026-2027 (solid-state battery integration)

**2. GPS-Denied Localization Accuracy**
- **Challenge**: Centimeter-level positioning in indoor/urban canyon environments
- **Current State**: ±5-10cm drift using SLAM/VIO (Visual-Inertial Odometry)
- **Target State**: ±2mm absolute accuracy for steel placement and prefab assembly
- **Who's Working On It**: Trimble (RTS series), Dusty Robotics, University of Michigan (APRIL Lab)
- **Estimated Timeline**: 2025-2026 (Ultra-wideband + visual fusion maturation)

**3. Heterogeneous Material Grasping**
- **Challenge**: Handling irregular construction materials (rebar bundles, bags, rough lumber)
- **Current State**: <15% of construction material types manipulable by current grippers
- **Target State**: >80% material handling versatility (comparable to human hand)
- **Who's Working On It**: MIT CSAIL (GelSight tactile sensors), RightHand Robotics, SRI International
- **Estimated Timeline**: 2027-2028 (General-purpose dexterous manipulation)

**4. Weather Hardening**
- **Challenge**: IP ratings insufficient for construction dust/moisture
- **Current State**: IP54 standard (limited dust/water protection); 15% failure rate in heavy rain
- **Target State**: IP67 with self-cleaning sensor housings; <1% weather-related downtime
- **Who's Working On It**: FBR, Construction Robotics (SAM100), Norwegian University of Science and Technology
- **Estimated Timeline**: 2025-2026

**5. Human-Robot Collaboration Safety**
- **Challenge**: Power/force limiting for heavy construction robots (>500kg)
- **Current State**: Safety-rated monitored stop (ISO 10218-2) requires 2m separation
- **Target State**: Speed and separation monitoring allowing collaborative masonry (<10cm proximity)
- **Who's Working On It**: ABB Robotics, KUKA (iiwa construction variants), TUM (Technical University of Munich)
- **Estimated Timeline**: 2026 (ISO/TS 15066 construction-specific amendments expected)
